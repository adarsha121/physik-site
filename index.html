About Biophysics

Biophysics applies the principles of physics to biological systems. It seeks to understand the physical mechanisms underlying biological processes at all scales, from molecules to organisms. This field is crucial for understanding how life works, from the folding of proteins to the functioning of the nervous system.

Molecular Dynamics (MD) Simulations

Molecular Dynamics simulations are computational methods used to study the physical movements of atoms and molecules. By solving Newton's equations of motion, MD simulations provide insights into the dynamic behavior of biological systems over time. These simulations are essential for understanding protein folding, drug interactions, and membrane dynamics.

Applications of MD Simulations

MD simulations have a wide range of applications in biophysics and related fields, including:

  • Drug Discovery: Predicting how drugs bind to target molecules.
  • Protein Folding: Studying the process by which proteins achieve their functional shape.
  • Membrane Dynamics: Investigating the behavior of cell membranes and their interactions with proteins and other molecules.
  • Enzyme Catalysis: Understanding the mechanisms by which enzymes speed up chemical reactions.
  • DNA Interactions: Exploring how proteins interact with DNA.
